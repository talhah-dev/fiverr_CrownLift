What is mudjacking?

A safe method to level misaligned slabs

Mudjacking (concrete lifting) is done by drilling small access holes into the slab and pumping a sand-and-cement mixture underneath. The material fills voids and gently raises the concrete into position, then holes are patched cleanly.

Stable fill

Fills voids beneath the slab to reduce movement.

Water resistant

A dense repair that handles moisture well.

Common causes of settlement

Erosion & washout

Water moves soil away and leaves voids beneath slabs.

Freeze/thaw

Seasonal movement can shift slabs and open gaps.

Unstable soil

Compaction changes over time and slabs settle.
