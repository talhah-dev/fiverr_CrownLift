GENERAL

Mudjacking (concrete lifting) levels misaligned slabs by drilling small access holes and pumping a sand-and-cement mix beneath the slab. It fills voids and raises the concrete back into position, then the holes are patched.

Sidewalks, driveways, patios, stoops/porches, steps, garage floors, pool decks, basement floors, curbs, and many other free-floating slabs.

PRICING & TIMING

Pricing varies by slab size, how far it has settled, accessibility, and how many areas need lifting. It’s commonly more cost-effective than replacement. Share details for an accurate estimate.

Many projects are completed within a few hours. Because you’re not pouring a new slab, there’s typically no long cure time like full replacement.

In many cases, surfaces can be used shortly after the lift and patching. We’ll confirm the best timing for your specific job on-site.

DURABILITY & OPTIONS

Mudjacking is a long-lasting repair because it fills voids beneath the slab. Long-term results also depend on drainage, soil stability, and preventing erosion—addressing root causes helps it last.

Mudjacking uses a sand-and-cement mix to fill voids and lift concrete. Foam lifting uses polyurethane foam. The best choice depends on the slab, soil conditions, and project goals—we’ll recommend what fits your situation.
